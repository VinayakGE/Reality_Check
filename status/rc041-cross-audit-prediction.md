## Two-Level Prediction Structure

This prediction has two levels that must be distinguished:

**Level 1 — Composite regularity prediction:**
Intermediate maturity → APG primary, Watch B secondary. Same as RC-037 and RC-040. The gradient regularity makes this prediction independently of CMech-001.

**Level 2 — CMech-001 differential prediction:**
Within the Intermediate composite class, domains with High DAM and High SRC should show Watch B more weakly suppressed than domains with Low or Intermediate DAM and SRC. Air pollution's High DAM (named attribution methodology with formal IARC certification) and High SRC (directional consensus) should have suppressed Watch B more effectively than the natural experiment literature alone. CMech-001 predicts Watch B intensity in RC-041 should be lower than in RC-037 (DAM=Low) and RC-040 (DAM=Intermediate), despite identical composite classifications.

**The diagnostic test:** If Watch B is minimal (as in the High-maturity domains RC-036 and RC-039) despite the Intermediate composite score, CMech-001's Prediction 2 is supported: DAM and SRC drive Watch B suppression, not NEA or composite score. If Watch B is secondary (as in RC-037 and RC-040), CMech-001's differential prediction fails — the criterion profile mismatch did not produce differential Watch B strength.

## Why This Selection Tests CMech-001

CMech-001 claims that causal-identification capacity (not composite maturity score) determines whether Watch B is feasible as an active research frontier. If the mechanism is correct, what matters most for Watch B suppression is DAM and SRC — because these directly certify that a research community has reached formal consensus on causal direction. NEA provides the underlying evidence; DAM codifies it into a named methodology; SRC aggregates it into formal consensus; RD confirms it across teams.

Air pollution has High DAM — the IARC Group 1 classification and GBD methodology are exactly the kind of formal causal certification that CMech-001 predicts suppresses Watch B. If Watch B is stronger in RC-041 than in High-maturity domains despite High DAM and SRC, one of two conclusions follows: either (a) NEA is the critical Watch B-suppressing criterion (not DAM), or (b) it is the composite score that matters, not any individual criterion.

Either conclusion is informative about CMech-001.

## What Would Constitute a Wound to CMech-001

**Watch B as strong secondary (same intensity as RC-037 or RC-040):** CMech-001's Prediction 2 fails. DAM=High and SRC=High did not produce differential Watch B suppression compared to domains with lower DAM and SRC at the same composite level.

**Watch B as primary:** CMech-001 and the composite regularity both fail. Watch B is dominant in an Intermediate-composite domain with High DAM — the most strongly wound scenario.

**APG primary + Watch B minimal (matching High-maturity domains):** CMech-001's Prediction 2 is confirmed. The criterion profile (High DAM, High SRC) suppressed Watch B despite the Intermediate composite, supporting the mechanism over the regularity alone.
