# RC-044 Cross-Audit Prediction

**Date recorded:** 2026-06-24
**Status:** Pre-registered — audit not yet conducted

This prediction is the tenth cross-audit ontology prediction and the seventh under independent predictor measurement. Its specific role: discriminate between the two interpretations of the RC-043 Wound 6 partial result.

---

## The Discriminant Function

RC-043 produced Theoretical (optimum) Watch B under High DAM. Two interpretations are consistent with that result:

**Interpretation A — DAM left the optimum question open.**
The GBD comparative risk assessment computed a TMREL and burden estimate, but did not explicitly adjudicate the J-curve debate. It assumed a TMREL and computed downstream. Theoretical (optimum) Watch B survived because DAM never directly engaged the optimum question — it presupposed an answer. On this interpretation, DAM that explicitly addressed the optimum question would suppress Theoretical (optimum) Watch B.

**Interpretation B — DAM cannot suppress optimum-level Watch B even when it engages the question directly.**
DAM formalizes causal attribution. Formalization is not resolution. A named methodology that takes an explicit institutional position on the optimal level still does not settle the theoretical dispute among researchers who reject that position's premises. On this interpretation, Theoretical (optimum) Watch B is a feature of the question type, not a gap in any particular DAM implementation.

RC-043 cannot distinguish these interpretations. Alcohol can.

**Why alcohol discriminates:** The GBD 2018 comparative risk assessment (Griswold et al., *The Lancet*) explicitly argued that the correct TMREL for alcohol is zero — that after correcting for sick-quitter confounding, the apparent J-curve disappears and no protective level can be identified. This is the most direct possible DAM engagement with the optimum question: not merely setting a TMREL as a methodological input, but defending TMREL=0 as the substantive conclusion of the attribution analysis.

If Theoretical (optimum) Watch B persists in the alcohol domain despite GBD's explicit TMREL=0 position: Interpretation B is supported. Formalization ≠ resolution. The scope boundary is genuine and survives direct engagement.

If Watch B is Methodological only: Interpretation A is supported. RC-043 was showing incomplete DAM engagement, not a general scope limitation. The scope story requires substantial revision.

## Critical Diagnostic Uncertainty

This prediction cannot specify Watch B character in advance because the RC-044 discriminant function is precisely about which character appears under explicit DAM engagement with the optimum question.

**If Watch B is Theoretical (optimum) — Interpretation B supported:**
Theoretical (optimum) Watch B persists despite GBD's explicit TMREL=0 argument. DAM can formalize an optimum position; formalization does not resolve the dispute. The scope boundary identified in RC-043 is real and survives a direct test. RC-043 was not showing incomplete DAM implementation — it was showing a genuine limitation of what DAM can accomplish.

**If Watch B is Methodological only — Interpretation A supported:**
GBD's explicit sick-quitter correction and TMREL=0 defense suppressed Theoretical (optimum) Watch B. RC-043's Theoretical (optimum) Watch B was an artifact of DAM leaving the TMREL underspecified rather than defending it. The RC-043 scope story requires revision: the boundary is at DAM completeness, not DAM scope.

**If Watch B is Theoretical (optimum) + Methodological simultaneously:**
Same structure as RC-043. Both interpretations remain viable. RC-044 does not discriminate. The J-curve debate has a methodological dimension (sick-quitter confounding) and a theoretical dimension (HDL-protective mechanism is biologically real) that co-exist regardless of DAM's formal position. Less informative than either clean outcome, but preserves all prior interpretations.

## Why This Is More Discriminating Than RC-043

RC-043 was the Wound 6 test. RC-044 is not a wound test — Wound 6 was not triggered. RC-044 tests which of two interpretations explains the RC-043 result.

The mechanism scope delimitation phase is asking a narrower and more specific question than the mechanism-decomposition phase. RC-044 does not ask whether DAM transforms Watch B. It asks what limits DAM's transformation capacity: incomplete engagement or structural incapacity.

That distinction has direct implications for CMech-001's scope refinement. If Interpretation B is supported, the refinement stands and the mechanism's boundaries are now specified. If Interpretation A is supported, the mechanism is intact with a smaller revision: DAM that explicitly addresses the optimum question transforms all Watch B types, including Theoretical (optimum).
