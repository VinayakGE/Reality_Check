## The Primary Instructions for This Checkpoint

Two distinct things must be held together without merging them.

**First:** The four-cell discovery timing map that emerged across RC-030–RC-033 is an observation tool, not a causal model. The moment the repository describes discovery timing as *explaining* outcomes rather than *classifying* implementation processes, it has taken a step the evidence has not yet supported. The cells can be used to organize observations. They cannot be used to predict outcomes until at least one cell occupies a position where the classification was made before the outcome was known, and the prediction holds.

**Second:** The attribution precision gap has reached three cross-domain instances (RC-023, RC-028, RC-034). That threshold requires the checkpoint to specify its relationship to Watch B explicitly — not to promote it, but to prevent it from silently becoming a different object than Watch B without the distinction being recorded. Three instances in three domains with a coherent structure is not coincidence. It is also not an established pattern. The checkpoint records the distinction and defers the promotion question.

## Three Repository-Level Observations from Cycle 5

These are observations about the evidence environment and the repository's operating constraints, not findings about any individual claim.

**Observation 1 — Discovery timing is an observation dimension, not an explanatory model**

The four-cell map documents how implementations differ in when and whether discovery of assumption mismatches occurs. It is descriptively useful. Its explanatory claim — that discovery timing causally influences outcomes — has not been validated. The cells would need to predict outcomes in cases where the classification is made before outcomes are known. That test has not been run. Until it is, the four cells classify; they do not explain.

**Observation 2 — Implementation-process observability may be a structural constraint**

RC-033 encountered a gap that was not about causal uncertainty (Watch B) and not about contribution shares (Attribution Precision Gap). It was about the object under study — the discovery process — being systematically underdocumented in the implementation literature. Outcome data was abundant; process data was absent. If this is a general property of complex program evaluation literature, it imposes a ceiling on what the discovery-timing hypothesis can be tested against. One instance is insufficient to establish this as a structural constraint. But it should be tracked: if subsequent audits repeatedly encounter the same implementation-documentation gap, the evidence environment constraint becomes a finding in its own right.

**Observation 3 — Attribution precision gap is now a cross-domain pattern requiring specification**

Three instances across three domains:

| Instance | Domain | Level | Bottleneck form |
|---|---|---|---|
| RC-023 (ORT) | Child survival | Transfer context | Concurrent programs at transfer site |
| RC-028 (Ethiopia HEP) | Community health | Transfer context | Concurrent programs at transfer site |
| RC-034 (Green Revolution) | Agricultural development | Source context | Concurrent inputs in foundational claim |

The gap's structure is consistent: outcome documented and accepted, cause accepted to have contributed, contribution share among concurrent causes indeterminate. This is not Watch B.

The distinction that requires explicit recording before Cycle 6:

*Watch B:* Outcome visible. Cause uncertain. Multiple competing explanations for what caused the outcome, with genuine dispute about whether the credited cause had any causal role.

*Attribution Precision Gap:* Outcome visible. Cause accepted (the credited cause contributed). Contribution share uncertain. Multiple concurrent causes were present; their relative contributions cannot be decomposed from available variation. The dispute is not about whether a cause was present but about how much of the outcome is attributable to it versus concurrent factors.

These require different evidence to resolve. Watch B requires causal identification studies that can establish whether the credited cause had any effect. Attribution precision gap requires decomposition studies with variation across input dimensions that allow estimation of relative contributions. They are different evidential problems, not the same problem at different intensities.

Promotion decision: The attribution precision gap is not promoted to a standalone Watch item at this checkpoint. Its relationship to Watch B must be specified (subtype vs. separate category) before it stands independently. The checkpoint records the distinction and the three-instance pattern; Cycle 6 should determine whether a fourth instance and further specification warrant promotion.

## The AGRA Implication from RC-034

This requires careful framing. It is easy to overstate.

What RC-034 established: The Green Revolution's success in Asia cannot be cleanly attributed primarily to HYV seeds because seeds performed well only in the context of simultaneous complementary input expansion (irrigation, fertilizer, rural credit, price support, extension). The contribution shares are indeterminate. The object "Green Revolution" is more accurately described as a package than as a seed technology.

What this means for RC-025 (AGRA): Before RC-034, the comparison baseline was: "Asian Green Revolution ≈ HYV seeds; AGRA tried to replicate seeds in Africa; transfer fell short." After RC-034, the baseline becomes: "Asian Green Revolution ≈ seeds + complementary input system; AGRA replicated only the seed component; this was not the full model." The object being transferred by AGRA was not the Green Revolution; it was an attributed simplification of the Green Revolution.

What this does NOT mean: RC-034 does not validate the selector explanation or prove that the complementary input system was the decisive factor. It changes the description of the source object. The selector explanation remains a candidate. The change is: the source case now looks more like a package than a technology, which is consistent with selector logic (what transfers is not the seed but the prerequisite package). Consistency is not confirmation.

## What Cycle 5 Did Not Earn

One sentence: Cycle 5 produced stronger evidence about the limits of evaluation than about the correctness of any particular explanation.

The discovery-timing thread did not validate adaptation or discovery infrastructure. It established that:
- Strong selector's predictions can be tested and failed
- Weak selector survives but on narrower ground

- The mechanism underlying the residual cannot be directly observed from the available evidence environment

The attribution precision gap thread did not validate any explanation of why complex interventions fail to transfer cleanly. It established that:
- The bottleneck appears at both source and transfer levels
- The bottleneck is structurally distinct from cause-uncertainty bottlenecks
- Three domains are enough to track; not enough to name as a standalone pattern

- Discovery infrastructure needs a second instance; one audit observation (MST Norway) is not a pattern.

**From Stream B (attribution precision gap):**

- The gap's relationship to Watch B requires formal specification. The checkpoint records the distinction; Cycle 6 should determine whether they are a subtype relationship (attribution precision gap ⊂ Watch B) or a parallel family with separate evidentiary requirements.
- A fourth instance would strengthen the cross-domain pattern. The question for audit selection: does the fourth instance need to add domain diversity, or should it add a specific variant (e.g., a case where the contribution share IS successfully decomposed, which would be a contrary case)?

**From the meta-level:**

- The implementation-documentation limitation is a one-instance observation. Track it: if a second audit encounters the same gap, it begins to be a structural constraint. If Cycle 6 audits proceed without encountering it, it remains idiosyncratic to BRAC's publication practices.
- The circularity concern from earlier checkpoints (availability values estimated from narrative + outcomes) has not been resolved. Cycle 6 should seek at least one case where availability is assessed from independent evidence before outcome classification.
- OQ-002 (longitudinal claim handling): RC-014's December 2027 deadline is approaching and no revision protocol exists. Cycle 6 should either develop the protocol or formally designate one audit as the first longitudinal claim review test case.
