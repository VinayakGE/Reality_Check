## The Primary Instruction for This Checkpoint

Resist compression.

The repository is at a stage where several observations are moving in the same direction (RC-027, RC-028, RC-029 all consistent with selector logic). That creates a temptation to compress the emerging explanation into a settled object and move on.

The checkpoint's job is to record what has actually been earned at the correct evidentiary level, and to preserve distinctions that will otherwise be lost to narrative gravity.

---

## The Hierarchy of What Cycle 4 Earned

Three levels. Each is real. Only the first two are established.

**Level 1 — Established:**

Selector explanation survived a domain transfer. Global health (RC-027, RC-028) → fintech (RC-029). The wound condition for RC-029 was explicit and pre-registered: if South Africa (high coordination, mismatched prerequisites) succeeded, the selector explanation would be weakened. South Africa failed. The wound condition was not triggered.

**Level 2 — Established:**

Transfer outcomes tracked prerequisite-profile similarity better than coordination level alone. South Africa: high coordination + mismatched prerequisites → failure. Bangladesh/bKash: matched prerequisites + comparable or lower coordination → success. This is the within-RC-029 comparison that discriminates selector from multiplier.

**Level 3 — Emerging residual (one instance):**

Successful transfers often involved redesigning the intervention package rather than copying it. bKash in Bangladesh adapted M-Pesa's design to local prerequisites (BRAC Bank trust infrastructure, garment worker remittance patterns). India's UPI succeeded by building on a different technical base entirely. These are not confirmations of the selector model — they are a residual the selector model was not explicitly accounting for.

Level 3 matters because it changes the role of coordination in the model. Under the selector explanation as stated at RC-028: coordination = *matching* (identifying which prerequisites are available and aligning the package to them). Under the adaptation residual: coordination = *redesigning* (actively modifying the intervention package until it fits available prerequisites). These are different behaviors.

Level 3 has one instance. It is a residual, not a replacement for the selector explanation.

## The Smallest Set of Observations That Currently Requires Explanation

This is a better organizing question than "what is the current model."

Four observations form the core set:

**1. South Africa forcing case (RC-027):** ~85% technical availability + near-zero coordination → near-zero outcome. Same infrastructure + restored coordination → world's largest ART program. This forced the technical/conversion availability split.

**2. Ethiopia opposite-corner test (RC-028):** ~22% technical availability + high coordination → outperformed AGRA (~35% technical, lower coordination) as the multiplicative model predicted. The technical-only model cannot produce this ordering.

**3. M-Pesa transfer variance (RC-029):** Within a single audit, transfer success correlated with prerequisite-profile similarity more than coordination level. South Africa (high coordination, mismatched) failed; Bangladesh (matched, comparable coordination) succeeded.

**4. Successful replications involving redesign (RC-029 H3):** bKash and UPI succeeded not by replicating the Kenya M-Pesa model but by redesigning the intervention package to match local prerequisites.

The selector explanation accounts for observations 1, 2, and 3. Observation 4 is the residual — it exists because of what the selector explanation did not explicitly model. The adaptation refinement is the response to observation 4.

The checkpoint records this distinction because if observation 4 is merged into the selector explanation prematurely, the repository loses the ability to test whether adaptation is a genuine general mechanism or an artifact of the fintech domain.

## What the Multiplicative Chain Looks Like at End of Cycle 4

The multi-stage explanatory chain that emerged in RC-027 has been refined through RC-028 and RC-029:

**RC-027 version:**
```
Technical Resources × Coordination Multiplier → Availability → Outcome
```

**End of Cycle 4 version (refined, not promoted):**
```
Available Prerequisites
        ↓
Coordination (as matching or redesign)
        ↓
Intervention Package ↔ Prerequisites Aligned
        ↓
Conversion Availability
        ↓
Outcome
```

The distinction between matching and redesigning is the open question the adaptation residual introduces. The chain is more complex than RC-027's formulation, but the added complexity is earned — it accounts for what the simpler version could not explain.

Neither version is promoted as the repository's current theory. Both are candidate structures the evidence has not yet refuted.

## What Cycle 5 Inherits

**From the watch items:**
- Watch B stable. CE-1 holds across 5 cross-context inference cases. No new pressure needed immediately.
- Watch A structural counts: pilot-to-policy (6), cross-context inference (5). Both are large enough to support within-structure analysis.

**From the explanatory layer:**
- Selector explanation needs: a case where coordination was high and the package was matched to available prerequisites but transfer still failed (falsifier). The repository does not yet have this.
- Adaptation refinement needs: a second instance. One instance is insufficient to treat it as a stable pattern.
- Attribution precision gap needs: a third instance before it warrants its own label separate from Watch B.

**From the meta-level:**
- The circularity concern (availability estimated from narrative + outcome, then used to predict outcome) has not been resolved. The gradient model's calibration points remain vulnerable to this. Cycle 5 should seek at least one case where availability can be assessed from independent evidence before outcome classification.

**The phase:**
The repository remains in Phase 3 (Constraint Discovery) and is beginning a transition into something that does not yet have a name — the phase where competing candidate explanations are tested against cases specifically designed to distinguish them. This is a higher-order activity than pattern discovery (Phase 1), mechanism discovery (Phase 2), or constraint discovery (Phase 3). It may not need a label yet. But the selection criterion for audits has changed: the highest-value case is now one that discriminates between the adaptation refinement and the selector explanation as currently stated, rather than one that adds another confirming instance of either.
