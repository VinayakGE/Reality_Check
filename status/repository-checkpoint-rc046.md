# Repository Checkpoint #011 — After RC-046

**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Trigger:** RC-046 closes Cycle 8; Stage 5 structure clarified; two distinct experimental tracks identified.
**Purpose:** Freeze how the remaining unknowns are organized, not just what the repository knows.

This checkpoint differs from earlier checkpoints in kind. Checkpoints #001–#010 primarily recorded what the repository had learned. Checkpoint #011 records what the repository has learned about the structure of what it does not yet know. That is a different type of epistemic state — and the one most likely to be obscured by subsequent evidence accumulation if it is not frozen.

---

## Section 1: Stage 5 State

### What Stage 5 Is

Stage 5 is the transition from mechanism construction to mechanism competition. Prior stages established that a regularity exists (Stage 2), that it holds under independent operationalization (Stage 3), and that a candidate mechanism can explain it with sufficient precision to generate wound conditions (Stage 4). Stage 5 asks: is that mechanism actually the best explanation, or does a competing mechanism explain the same pattern equally well or better?

Stage 5 has a specific design requirement that prior stages did not: the audit must be chosen so that competing mechanisms generate distinguishable predictions, and both mechanisms must have the opportunity to lose. Audits that both mechanisms predict similarly provide no Stage 5 information regardless of the outcome.

### CMech-001 Current Status

**CMech-001:** As Dedicated Attribution Methodology (DAM) increases, Watch B character transforms — shifting from Theoretical (existence-level) toward APG-structured, Methodological, or absent.

**Evidential position:**

| Audit | DAM | PC | CMech-001 Prediction | Outcome | Discriminating? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RC-041 | High | High | Watch B Methodological | Confirmed | No (PC also high) |
| RC-043 | High | High | Watch B recedes at existence level | Confirmed (with optimum exception) | No (PC also high) |
| RC-044 | High (lean) | High | Watch B recedes at existence level | Confirmed (with premise exception) | No (PC also high) |
| RC-045 | Intermediate-High | High | Watch B minimal | Confirmed | No (formal cert absent, but PC high) |
| **RC-046** | **Intermediate** | **Low** | **Existence Watch B absent** | **Confirmed** | **Yes — first Low PC domain** |

RC-046 is qualitatively different from RC-041 through RC-045: it is the first audit where CMech-001 and Alternative A made genuinely distinguishable predictions, specified in advance, and one mechanism's prediction was not confirmed. That makes it the first true Stage 5 observation in the mechanism-competition sense.

### Alternative A Current Status

**Alternative A (revised):** Prestige coordination — the concentration of epistemic authority in networks of elite journals, citation clusters, funding bodies, and hiring structures — suppresses Watch B by marginalizing existence-level causal skepticism. Without prestige coordination, Watch B persists at the existence level regardless of DAM development.

**RC-046 result:** Alternative A predicted existence-level Watch B active in psychotherapy effectiveness (Low PC, Intermediate DAM). Existence-level Watch B was absent. Alternative A's prediction was not confirmed at the existence level. This is the first direct supporting observation for CMech-001 over Alternative A in conditions where they diverged.

**Alternative A residual:** Modality-superiority disputes (CBT vs. psychodynamic) have not converged in psychotherapy despite decades of head-to-head RCTs. This pattern does not appear in High PC domains at comparable intensity. Alternative A may correctly describe convergence at the modality level even where it cannot explain Watch B suppression at the existence level. This is a scope-limiting observation, not a falsification — and it is currently at n=1.

**Current standing:** CMech-001 leads. Alternative A survives in a reduced scope and must defend that scope against future observations. Both mechanisms remain live.

## Section 2: Two Independent Tracks

Stage 5 contains two structurally distinct open questions. Prior RSQ-002 entries noted them adjacently. This checkpoint separates them explicitly because they require fundamentally different experimental designs.

---

### Track 1 — Mechanism Competition

**Question:** Which mechanism better explains the observed regularity in Watch B character across domains?

**Competitors:**
- CMech-001 (DAM drives Watch B transformation)
- Narrowed Alternative A (prestige coordination suppresses Watch B)

**What this track assumes:** The regularity is real. DAM and prestige coordination are genuinely different variables. The question is which one does the causal work.

**Current experimental status:** Functioning. Discriminating design exists (separate DAM from PC; choose domain where both have opportunity to win). RC-045 and RC-046 have begun producing observations. Both mechanisms have wound conditions. CMech-001 currently leads.

**What Track 1 needs next:** Another Low PC + Intermediate/High DAM domain to move the level-differentiated candidate refinement (DAM suppresses existence Watch B; PC affects modality convergence) from n=1 to an assessable pattern. Alternatively, a High PC + Low DAM domain (if such a domain exists) that would test Alternative A's mechanism independently of CMech-001.

---

### Track 2 — Artifact Challenge

**Question:** Is the regularity in Watch B character a property of the world, or a property of how research programs evolve and select for their own evidence base?

**Competitor:** Alternative B (selection into measurement)

**What this track challenges:** The assumption Track 1 makes. Alternative B does not offer a different mechanism for why Watch B behaves as it does; it challenges whether the Watch B gradient reflects genuine epistemic structure in the world or is an artifact of how mature research programs produce and consolidate their own evidence.

The distinction matters: if mature domains systematically attract more rigorous methods *and* produce more settled causal conclusions *because they started with stronger evidence*, then CMech-001's mechanism (DAM causes Watch B transformation) could be directionally correct while the causal arrow runs the other way (strong evidence attracts DAM, rather than DAM clarifying weak evidence).

**Current experimental status:** Conceptually active; experimentally undefined.

| Dimension | Track 1 | Track 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Discriminating design exists | Yes | No |
| Wound condition specified | Yes (both mechanisms) | No |
| Committed test | RC-046 and future audits | None |
| Can the alternative lose? | Yes | No |

**The asymmetry recorded here:** Alternative B cannot currently lose. It is not possible, using standard RC audit architecture, to produce an observation that would require revising or abandoning the artifact interpretation. This does not make Alternative B wrong. It makes it currently unfalsifiable within the repository's existing methodology.

**Design criterion for Track 2 (placeholder):** A valid Track 2 experiment must produce observations that differ depending on whether the Watch B gradient reflects evidential structure in the world or historical research-program selection. Standard single-claim audits are insufficient for this purpose because they operate within research programs rather than across the conditions that generated those programs.

This criterion does not prescribe a solution. It records that the existing RC audit architecture probably cannot answer Track 2, and that answering it would require a different type of investigation — possibly comparative study of how research programs with similar initial evidence diverged in maturity, or analysis of domains where institutional circumstances changed the prestige structure without changing the evidence base.

## Section 4: What the Repository Has Demonstrated and Not Demonstrated at Checkpoint #011

### Demonstrated

1. A stable ontology of recurring epistemic objects (Watch B, APG, Counterfactual Unavailability, CMech-001) applies prospectively across domains it was not built on.
2. Cross-audit predictions hold under independent operationalization — maturity gradient, domain maturity, prestige coordination all scored before claim evidence is reviewed.
3. CMech-001 generates predictions that differ from at least one plausible competitor (Alternative A), and those predictions have begun to be tested.
4. RC-046 executed a correlation-breaking design: the first audit where DAM and PC were cleanly separated, where both mechanisms had opportunity to win, and where one mechanism's prediction was confirmed and one was not.
5. Watch B sub-type distinctions (Theoretical existence, Theoretical optimum, Methodological, Directional at modality level) carry more discriminating information than Watch B presence/absence.

### Not Demonstrated

1. That CMech-001 is the correct mechanism rather than the leading mechanism in an unfinished competition.
2. That the Watch B gradient is not an artifact of research program selection (Alternative B untested).
3. That the level-differentiated candidate refinement (DAM suppresses existence Watch B; PC affects modality convergence) replicates beyond RC-046.
4. That the repository framework improves real-world decisions or generates measurable operational value (Stage 6 untested).
5. That Track 2 is even addressable within the existing audit architecture.

## Section 5: Cycle 9 Entry Condition

Cycle 9 opens from here. Two legitimate paths forward:

**Path A — Continue Track 1:** Select a domain that satisfies one of the following discriminating profiles:
- Low PC + High DAM (test whether stronger DAM changes the level-differentiated pattern)
- High PC + Low-Intermediate DAM (test Alternative A's mechanism in isolation from CMech-001)
- A second Low PC + Intermediate/High DAM domain that could either replicate RC-046 or produce a different Watch B character (moving the level-differentiated candidate toward or away from promotion)

Any Cycle 9 Track 1 audit must commit pre-registered maturity and PC scores before claim evidence is reviewed.

**Path B — Design Track 2:** Specify what a discriminating Track 2 experiment would look like — what data it would require, what observation would support Alternative B over the reality interpretation, and what observation would pressure it. This is not another audit; it is a methodology problem. A Track 2 design document would itself be a repository contribution, even if execution is deferred.

**No constraint on sequencing:** Paths A and B are not mutually exclusive. Cycle 9 could pursue both. The checkpoint records the choice point; it does not prescribe the route.
