## Critical Diagnostic Uncertainty

This prediction cannot specify Watch B character in advance because the Wound 6 test is precisely about which character Watch B takes. The prediction commits only to Watch B's presence; its character is the test output.

**If Watch B is Methodological:** The sodium → blood pressure mechanism is formally accepted; concerns are about cohort confounding and dose-response measurement, not competing causal theories. High DAM (GBD comparative risk assessment) suppressed Theoretical Watch B even with Intermediate SRC. Revised CMech-001 survives. DAM alone may be sufficient for the transformation independent of SRC.

**If Watch B is Theoretical:** The PURE J-curve and dose-response shape debate constitute live competing theoretical claims (low sodium may be harmful; optimal sodium may be higher than WHO recommends), and High DAM did not suppress them. Wound 6 is inflicted. The revised CMech-001's claim that DAM drives Watch B transformation is directly challenged.

**If Watch B is Mixed (both Methodological and Theoretical simultaneously):** This would be a structurally new finding — Watch B with two concurrent characters. The table's column would need a revision to accommodate co-occurrence. Neither pure support nor pure wound for Wound 6.

## Why This Is More Diagnostic Than RC-041 or RC-042

RC-041 (Air Pollution): High DAM + High SRC → Methodological Watch B. Two criteria supporting transformation simultaneously — cannot isolate DAM's independent contribution.

RC-042 (Incarceration): High NEA + Low DAM → Theoretical Watch B. NEA without DAM insufficient.

RC-043 (Dietary Sodium): High DAM + Intermediate SRC → Watch B character unknown. Tests DAM's independent contribution when SRC is not as strong as in RC-041. If DAM alone produces Methodological Watch B here, it is confirmed as the transformation driver independent of SRC.
