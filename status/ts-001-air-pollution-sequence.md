# TS-001: Air Pollution Research Program — Historical Sequence Analysis

**Date conducted:** 2026-06-25
**Pre-registration:** `status/track-2-experimental-architecture.md` (committed before this investigation)
**Investigation type:** Track 2 sequence analysis — not a current-state audit
**Domain:** Long-term PM2.5 exposure and premature mortality (RC-041 domain)

---

## Purpose and Design Recalled

The Track 2 question is not "what is Watch B's current character?" but "in what order did Watch B transformation and DAM introduction occur?"

**CMech-001 historical component predicts:** DAM introduction preceded Watch B transformation. Where DAM was introduced at an identifiable moment, Watch B character should be different after than before — with the underlying causal evidence held approximately constant between the two points.

**Alternative B predicts:** Watch B transformation preceded or co-occurred with DAM introduction. The research program converged on a causal conclusion because of accumulating evidence quality; DAM (IARC classification, GBD methodology) formalized that convergence rather than producing it.

**Pre-registered wound conditions (from track-2-experimental-architecture.md):**
- CMech-001 wounded (at historical component) if: Watch B transformation preceded DAM introduction; DAM was introduced to formalize an existing consensus
- Alternative B wounded if: Watch B was Theoretical before DAM introduction and became Methodological/APG after it, with the underlying evidence base not substantially changing between those points

---

## Methodological Notes for Historical Reconstruction

Watch B character is assessed at each time point based on what questions were being actively prosecuted as research-program disputes — not what we now know the answers to be. A question counts as Watch B-active at a given period if credentialed researchers were publishing challenges to the causal claim, not merely noting uncertainty.

DAM presence is assessed based on whether named institutional methodology existed and was being applied — not merely whether individual studies used rigorous methods. Individual rigorous studies are not DAM; named frameworks applied systematically across a domain to produce authoritative estimates are DAM.

## Timeline Reconstruction

### Period 1: Pre-1993 — Foundational Epidemiology

**Evidence base:** Early studies of total suspended particles (TSP) and respiratory disease. Cross-sectional associations documented in UK and US settings. Early cohort studies beginning. Studies use TSP, not PM2.5 specifically. Confounding by socioeconomic status, smoking, and occupational exposures was not well controlled.

**Watch B character:** Theoretical (existence-level) active. The core questions being prosecuted as research-program disputes: Is the association real or an artifact of confounding? Are particle concentrations at ambient levels — well below occupational thresholds — actually harmful? The causal chain from ambient particles to mortality was contested at the existence level. No major review body endorsed a causal relationship; EPA standards were based on cautious precautionary reasoning rather than established causal attribution.

**DAM present:** No. No named framework for PM2.5 specifically. No institutional body applying systematic causal attribution methodology. Individual epidemiological studies exist but no authoritative attribution mechanism.

**Assessment:** Theoretical Watch B active, DAM absent.

---

### Period 2: 1993–2002 — Harvard Six Cities Era and the Reanalysis

**Key events:**
- 1993: Dockery et al. (*NEJM*) — Harvard Six Cities Study. Prospective cohort of 8,111 adults in six US cities over 14–16 years. PM2.5 specifically measured. Hazard ratio of 1.26 (95% CI 1.08–1.47) for most polluted vs. least polluted city. First major study showing PM2.5 specifically associated with all-cause mortality.
- 1995: Pope et al. — American Cancer Society (ACS) cohort, 500,000 adults, confirmed and extended Harvard findings.
- 1997: EPA promulgates first PM2.5 National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS), citing these studies as key evidence.
- 1997–2002: Health Effects Institute (HEI) commissions an independent reanalysis of both the Harvard Six Cities and ACS data specifically in response to critiques that the original analyses were unreplicable and might reflect confounding. The Krewski et al. reanalysis team (2000, 2003) confirms the original findings with access to the original data.

**Watch B character:** Theoretical (existence-level) active, but contested — and the intensity of contention tells us something. The HEI reanalysis was commissioned *because* Watch B was active: industry groups and some academic critics argued the findings could not be independently verified (the original data had not been released), that the effect estimates might be confounded, and that PM2.5 at ambient concentrations might not be causally harmful. The EPA standard itself was legally challenged. Watch B was not merely present as routine scientific caution; it was an active research-program dispute visible in published critiques, Congressional testimony, and the commissioning of the HEI reanalysis.

By 2002 (HEI reanalysis confirming original findings), Watch B was under pressure but not suppressed. Multiple independent cohorts were showing consistent results, but the existence-level question was not yet settled in the sense that critiques continued to appear.

**DAM present:** Partial emergence. Named prospective cohort methodology existed. PM2.5 was established as the specific pollutant of interest. But no systematic institutional framework for causal attribution existed; no IARC classification; no GBD application. Individual studies were rigorous but the field lacked a named mechanism for authoritative attribution.

**Assessment:** Theoretical Watch B active (declining), DAM in early development (named methodology for individual studies; no institutional application).

---

### Period 3: 2003–2012 — Replication Accumulation and Declining Existence-Level Watch B

**Key events:**
- 2002–2006: Multiple independent cohort studies from Europe, Canada, and other US populations. Laden et al. (2006) extended Six Cities follow-up with additional 8 years; found mortality reductions in cities with improving PM2.5, providing quasi-experimental evidence.
- 2007–2010: Meta-analyses of cohort studies published. Pope and Dockery review (2006, *JAPCA*) synthesizes evidence across many cohorts. Consistent effect estimates emerging across independent populations.
- 2009–2012: Low-concentration studies beginning to examine the concentration-response function below EPA standards. Indoor air, biomass burning, and occupational exposure studies contributing to understanding of the concentration-response curve.
- 2010: Health Effects Institute Panel on the Health Effects of Traffic-Related Air Pollution — systematic review establishing causal inference for near-road PM2.5.

**Watch B character:** During this period, existence-level Watch B declined substantially. Multiple independent replications across different countries, different cohorts, and different study designs all converged on similar effect estimates. Published critiques of the existence of an effect became rare in peer-reviewed literature. The active frontier of dispute shifted toward: What is the shape of the concentration-response function at lower concentrations? Is there a threshold below which PM2.5 has no effect? Which components of PM2.5 are most harmful?

The Watch B transformation — from Theoretical (existence-level) to Methodological/APG — appears to have occurred primarily in this period, before the major DAM events (IARC 2013, GBD comparative risk assessment 2013–2014).

**DAM present:** Emerging but not yet institutionalized. Named cohort methodology existed; systematic reviews were produced; meta-analytic synthesis was available. But no single institutional framework had applied the methodology to produce authoritative global estimates. EPA NAAQS process represented one form of systematic review, but not a named global attribution mechanism.

**Assessment:** Watch B transitioning Theoretical→Methodological/APG, primarily in this period. DAM developing but not yet institutionally applied in the form of named global attribution.

**Critical observation for Track 2:** The bulk of the Watch B transformation appears to have occurred during Period 3, driven by evidence accumulation across independent cohorts, before the major identifiable DAM events of 2013–2014.

---

### Period 4: 2013–2015 — IARC Classification and GBD Methodology

**Key events:**
- October 2013: IARC classifies outdoor air pollution (and PM2.5 specifically) as Group 1 carcinogen — sufficient evidence of carcinogenicity in humans. This is the most prominent formal institutional classification event in the domain.
- 2013: GBD 2010 published (Murray et al., *The Lancet*). Global Burden of Disease applies comparative risk assessment methodology to ambient PM2.5, generating the first systematic global estimates of PM2.5-attributable mortality burden (3.2 million deaths annually attributed to ambient PM2.5 in 2010).
- 2014: Burnett et al. develop the Integrated Exposure-Response (IER) function, synthesizing ambient, indoor air, second-hand smoke, and active smoking data to model the concentration-response relationship across a wide range of exposures. This is the key DAM tool for generating burden estimates.
- 2015: GBD 2013 and GBD 2015 publications refining estimates and methodology.

**Watch B character:** By the time of IARC 2013 and GBD 2010/2013, Watch B at the existence level was already substantially suppressed. The IARC classification was not unexpected; it formalized a consensus that had been developing through Period 3. After 2013, Watch B character is primarily:
- APG: How many deaths? What is the TMREL? What is the IER shape at very low concentrations?
- Methodological: Is the IER function valid for extrapolating from high-dose studies to ambient concentrations? How should PM2.5 composition be handled?

**DAM present:** Full institutional application. IARC Group 1, GBD comparative risk assessment, IER function methodology — all represent named frameworks applied by institutions to produce authoritative estimates. This is the clearest DAM period.

**Assessment:** Watch B now APG primary, Methodological secondary. Existence-level Watch B essentially absent. DAM fully developed and institutionally applied.

---

### Period 5: 2016–Present — Low-Concentration Frontier and Refined APG

**Key events:**
- 2017–2021: Di et al., Dominici group, and Vodonos et al. studies extending concentration-response function to very low PM2.5 concentrations (below 12 μg/m³ US standard, down to 2–3 μg/m³). Active dispute about whether effect estimates at these concentrations are confounded or reflect genuine effects.
- 2018–2020: Pope et al. (2019) and other studies refining the IER function.
- 2019: Global Burden of Disease 2019 — refined estimates, new TMREL methodology.
- 2021: HEI ISEE Global Exposure-Mortality Model (GEMM) — updated functional form for concentration-response.

**Watch B character:** APG primary (TMREL, concentration-response shape at very low concentrations, composition-specific effects). Methodological Watch B active (confounding at very low concentrations; validity of studies using Medicare data). No credentialed research program arguing that PM2.5 at typical urban concentrations is causally harmless.

**DAM present:** Mature and actively applied. Multiple competing institutional frameworks (GBD, GEMM, HEI systematic reviews) all using named methodology.

## Temporal Ordering Assessment

| Period | Watch B character | DAM status |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-1993 | Theoretical (existence-level) active | Absent |
| 1993–2002 | Theoretical active, declining | Early development (individual studies) |
| 2003–2012 | Transitioning Theoretical → Methodological/APG | Developing (no institutional application) |
| 2013–2015 | APG primary, Methodological secondary; existence absent | Fully developed (IARC, GBD, IER) |
| 2016–present | APG primary; existence absent | Mature (multiple competing frameworks) |

**The critical observation:** The Watch B transformation — from Theoretical (existence-level) to Methodological/APG — appears to have occurred primarily in Period 3 (2003–2012), driven by accumulation of multiple independent cohort replications. The major DAM events (IARC 2013 Group 1 classification; GBD 2013 comparative risk assessment; IER function 2014) occurred in Period 4, after the bulk of the Watch B transformation.

---

## Track 2 Result

**Against CMech-001 historical component:** The temporal ordering does not support CMech-001 at the existence-level Watch B transition. Existence-level Watch B declined primarily through independent evidence accumulation (multiple cohorts converging in Period 3), before institutional DAM was applied in Period 4. DAM introduction (IARC 2013, GBD 2013–2014) did not precede the Watch B transformation; it followed and formalized it.

This is a direct observation of the pattern Alternative B predicts: strong initial evidence generated both (a) the Watch B suppression at the existence level and (b) the DAM development in institutional form. The DAM formalized the consensus rather than producing it.

**Partial support for CMech-001 at a different level:** The transition from Methodological Watch B to APG Watch B — the shift in the research frontier from "did we correctly implement the causal attribution?" to "how much, through which pathway, at what concentration?" — does appear to correspond to Period 4–5, when GBD methodology and the IER function were developed. If DAM (IER, GBD) transformed the question from Methodological to APG, this would be CMech-001 operating at a finer granularity than existence-level Watch B.

But this is a weaker claim: DAM transforming Methodological → APG is less diagnostic than DAM transforming Theoretical → Methodological, because the latter is the existence-level Watch B suppression CMech-001's core claim concerns.

---

## Track 2 Implications

**Alternative B receives first supporting observation.** In the air pollution research program, existence-level Watch B suppression preceded institutional DAM introduction. The sequence is:
1. Evidence accumulates (Period 2–3)
2. Watch B transforms from Theoretical to Methodological/APG (Period 3)
3. DAM is institutionally applied (Period 4)

This sequence is consistent with Alternative B: strong initial evidence (the Harvard Six Cities, ACS, and multiple independent replications) drove Watch B suppression; DAM (IARC, GBD) formalized the result.

**What this does not establish:**
- That CMech-001's cross-sectional and prospective components are wrong (TS-001 addresses only the historical component)
- That the air pollution pattern generalizes to other domains (n=1 for the Track 2 observation; anti-compression applies)
- That DAM played no role (DAM may have transformed Methodological → APG and enabled burden quantification even if it didn't suppress existence-level Watch B)

**Candidate Track 2 refinement (n=1; anti-compression applies):** DAM may operate differently at different Watch B transitions: evidence accumulation suppresses Theoretical Watch B; DAM transforms the residual Methodological frontier into APG structure. This is a level-differentiated Track 2 analog to the level-differentiated Track 1 candidate from RC-046. Both suggest DAM and evidence accumulation may have different roles at different stages of Watch B transformation. But n=1 in each case; these are candidates, not findings.

## What TS-002 Should Investigate

If the air pollution sequence analysis is to be assessed as more than a single observation, a second sequence analysis should examine a domain with different characteristics. Two candidates:

**Climate attribution (RC-036 domain):** The World Weather Attribution collaborative represents a clear DAM development event (formal event attribution methodology developed systematically after 2012). The causal claim (human influence on climate) has a potentially different sequence — the IPCC confidence levels increased progressively with evidence, and the formal methodology for single-event attribution came later. Does the same pattern hold (Watch B suppression preceded DAM institutionalization) or is this a case where DAM (single-event attribution) preceded the Watch B transformation for specific weather event claims?

**Vitamin A supplementation (RC-039 domain):** The DEVTA trial (2012) is a clear evidence event that challenged the prior consensus. Was the initial consensus formed before formal Cochrane/GBD methodology was applied, or did DAM (WHO guidelines, Cochrane meta-analysis) drive the initial Watch B suppression? This could test whether the air pollution sequence is typical or whether domains where formal institutional methodology appeared earlier show a different pattern.

A second Track 2 observation from a domain with clearly different DAM introduction timing would determine whether the air pollution sequence generalizes or represents one specific pattern among multiple possible orderings.
