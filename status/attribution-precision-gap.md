# Attribution Precision Gap

## Promoted Epistemic Structure Object

**Promoted:** 2026-06-24
**Promotion basis:** RSQ-001 candidate criteria (3 domains, structural consistency, core boundary specification)

---

## Definition

The Attribution Precision Gap describes a recurring bottleneck in evidence evaluation where:

- The outcome is documented and accepted
- The credited cause is accepted as a genuine contributor
- The contribution share of that cause among concurrent causes cannot be decomposed from available evidence

The dispute is not about whether a cause was present or had any effect. It is about how much of the outcome is attributable to it versus concurrent factors operating simultaneously.

---

## Distinction from Watch B

These are different evidential problems, not the same problem at different intensities.

| Object | Core question | Required evidence to resolve |
|---|---|---|
| Watch B | Did the credited cause have any causal role? | Causal identification studies that can establish whether the credited cause had any effect |
| Attribution Precision Gap | How much of the outcome belongs to this accepted cause versus concurrent others? | Decomposition studies with variation across input dimensions that allow estimation of relative contributions |

---

## Relationship to Watch B: Sequential vs. Parallel

The distinction above specifies what the two objects are. This section specifies how they relate when both are plausible within the same audit.

**The activation condition embedded in the APG definition:** APG requires that "the credited cause is accepted as a genuine contributor." This is not merely a criterion for classification — it is a logical precondition. If causal role is not yet accepted, the contribution-share question cannot be meaningfully posed. Watch B resolves (or remains unresolved) first; APG activates afterward.

**Sequential relationship (same causal question):**
When Watch B and APG both address the same causal question — "Did cause X contribute, and if so, how much?" — they are logically sequential, not parallel. Watch B must be provisionally resolved in favor of accepted causal role before APG can become the operative bottleneck. RC-035 (East Asian industrial policy) surfaced this structure: the debate centered on whether industrial policy had any independent causal role at all (Watch B), with contribution-share decomposition (APG) present as a conditional sub-question that depends on Watch B resolution.

**Parallel relationship (different causal questions):**
When Watch B and APG address different components of the same claim, they can be parallel. RC-023 and RC-028 show this: Watch B applies to one aspect of the attribution question while APG applies to a different aspect simultaneously. The two objects are not in a dependency relationship when they address distinct causal questions within the claim.

**What this means for classification:**
The unit of analysis is the causal question, not the claim. A single claim can contain multiple causal questions, each of which may independently be Watch B or APG. When those questions are about the same cause, the objects are sequential. When about different causes or different aspects of attribution, they can be parallel.

This relationship was made visible by RC-035 but is entailed by the definitions, not discovered empirically. It resolves OQ-Regional from the RC-035 audit.

## Instances

| Instance | Domain | Bottleneck level | Structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| RC-023 (ORT) | Child survival | Transfer context | Outcome accepted; ORT contribution accepted; concurrent programs (vaccines, nutrition, sanitation) at transfer site prevent attribution to ORT specifically |
| RC-028 (Ethiopia HEP) | Community health systems | Transfer context | Outcome accepted; HEP contribution accepted; concurrent factors (economic growth, urbanization, HDI improvements) prevent decomposition of HEP's share |
| RC-034 (Green Revolution) | Agricultural development | Source context | Yield gains accepted; HYV contribution accepted; concurrent complementary inputs (irrigation, fertilizer, credit, pricing, extension) prevent decomposition of HYV's share |

RC-034 introduced a variant not present in RC-023 or RC-028: the gap at the *source context level*, where the foundational claim itself is indeterminate rather than the transfer evaluation.

## Promotion Decision

Promoted under RSQ-001 candidate criteria:

**Criterion 1 — Instance count across domains:** Satisfied. Three distinct domains across two levels of the attribution problem.

**Criterion 2 — Structural consistency:** Satisfied. Across all three: outcome documented and accepted; credited cause accepted as contributor; contribution share among concurrent causes indeterminate. Bottleneck consistently located at decomposition, not cause identification.

**Criterion 3 — Boundary specification:** Core satisfied. Boundary from Watch B specified (see above). Boundary edges incomplete — see recorded incompleteness below.

---

## Recorded Boundary Incompleteness

The core Watch B / Attribution Precision Gap boundary is specified. Two edge conditions remain unresolved:

**Boundary Condition A — Intensity Threshold (Watch B ↔ Attribution Precision Gap)**

If a credited cause's accepted contribution is very small — present but estimated at a minor share — at what point does decomposition uncertainty shade into causal-role uncertainty? The three current instances have substantial accepted contributions. The first instance with a smaller, disputed contribution will sit at this boundary. Resolution requires: a case where contribution share is acceptably small, and explicit assessment of whether that smallness triggers Watch B reclassification.

**Boundary Condition B — Counterfactual Unavailability (Attribution Precision Gap ↔ Counterfactual Unavailability)**

Attribution Precision Gap assumes an observable outcome and accepted contributors; the question is contribution decomposition. Counterfactual unavailability concerns whether the necessary comparison world can exist or be observed at all. The relationship between these has not been specified. Resolution requires: explicit examination of a case that could plausibly fit either category, with criteria for distinguishing them.

---

## Evidence Requirements for Resolution

Resolving any specific Attribution Precision Gap instance requires decomposition studies with variation across input dimensions. Specifically:

- Sub-national or cross-condition comparisons where individual inputs vary while others are held roughly constant
- Experimental or quasi-experimental designs that allow relative contribution estimation
- Historical reconstructions with sufficient granularity to estimate marginal effects of individual inputs

These requirements are demanding. Some instances may be permanently unresolvable on current evidence (the Green Revolution case being a candidate — the necessary variation existed regionally but was not studied with this question in mind).

---

## Relationship to Repository Ledger

The Attribution Precision Gap is not an explanatory object. It does not compete with selector, adaptation, or coordination density to explain outcomes. It describes a structural limit on how precisely any explanation can be attributed in cases where concurrent causes were present and their contributions cannot be decomposed.

Finding an attribution precision gap in a case does not resolve the question of which cause mattered most. It establishes that the question cannot be answered from current evidence.
