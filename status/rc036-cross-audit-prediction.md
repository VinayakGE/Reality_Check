# RC-036 Cross-Audit Prediction

**Date recorded:** 2026-06-24
**Status:** Pre-registered — audit not yet conducted

This prediction was recorded before reviewing specific evidence on the 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome attribution literature. It constitutes the second cross-audit ontology prediction in the repository's history.

---

## Prediction

**Domain:** Climate Attribution Science

**Specific claim:** "Anthropogenic climate change significantly increased the probability and severity of the 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome."

**Predicted Primary Object:** Attribution Precision Gap

**Predicted Secondary Object:** Watch B

**Predicted Non-Appearance:** Counterfactual Unavailability

**Flagged candidate:** Probabilistic causation threshold (potential new object — see below)

**Reason:**

Climate attribution science is a mature field developed specifically to resolve Watch B-type questions. The methodological core of the field — fraction of attributable risk, probability ratio analysis, counterfactual climate model runs — was constructed because "did climate change cause this?" was the original bottleneck. The field has systematically moved causal-role questions toward resolution using explicit probabilistic methods.

This domain-level reasoning generates a different prediction than RC-035 (industrial policy), where Watch B was predicted as primary because causal-role disputes underlie apparent contribution-share questions. In climate attribution, the methodological apparatus has specifically been built to resolve Watch B, making APG the live scientific frontier: causal role broadly accepted, contribution magnitude actively debated.

The claim's own framing is notable: it does not assert "climate change caused the heat dome" (Watch B territory). It asserts "significantly increased the probability and severity" — a probabilistic framing that presupposes causal role and contests magnitude. This framing anticipates APG rather than Watch B.

Counterfactual unavailability is predicted absent because attribution science explicitly models the counterfactual world (without anthropogenic forcing) using climate models. The comparison is constructed rather than unavailable.

**Flagged candidate — Probabilistic Causation Threshold:**

Attribution science may generate a bottleneck the repository hasn't previously seen: dispute about what probability ratio constitutes sufficient evidence of causal attribution for policy or legal purposes. A claim that "climate change made this event 150x more probable" may be scientifically uncontested while remaining contested on the question of whether "150x more probable" constitutes "caused." This is neither Watch B (causal role disputed) nor APG (contribution shares disputed) — it is a dispute about the threshold between probabilistic contribution and causal attribution. Flag only; one observation cannot constitute a new object.

## What Makes This Prediction Diagnostic

**If APG appears as primary:** Confirms that domains with mature probabilistic causal methods systematically produce APG rather than Watch B. Suggests the Watch B/APG ordering in RC-035 may reflect domain-level evidence maturity, not just claim structure.

**If Watch B appears as primary:** Suggests public and policy discourse about climate attribution has not tracked the scientific literature's movement. Watch B persists in the evidence environment even when attribution science has methodologically addressed it. The boundary between scientific and non-scientific discourse would need to be specified.

**If Probabilistic Causation Threshold appears:** First candidate new object in Cycle 6. Requires examination of whether it is a subtype of Watch B, a subtype of APG, or a genuinely distinct object.
