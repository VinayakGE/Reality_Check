## 2. The Missing Mechanism

The domain-maturity hypothesis currently reads:

> Domains with high scores on NEA, DAM, SRC, and RD tend to show APG as the primary epistemic object. Domains with low scores on those criteria tend to show Watch B as primary.

The operationalization criteria are predictors. They are not mechanisms. The four criteria describe features of a domain's literature ecology. They do not specify what process connects literature maturity to bottleneck dominance.

**Candidate mechanism (not yet a repository object):**

As causal-identification capacity in a domain increases, uncertainty about whether the credited cause had any causal role at all decreases. Once that uncertainty falls below a threshold — because natural experiments have established causal presence, or because probabilistic attribution methods have quantified it — the live research frontier moves from "did it cause?" (Watch B) to "how much did it cause?" (APG).

If this mechanism is correct, the four operationalization criteria predict object dominance because they are proxies for causal-identification capacity. NEA measures whether natural experiments exist to establish causal presence; DAM measures whether domain-specific methods exist to do so; SRC and RD measure whether those methods have actually produced consensus on causal direction.

This is a coherent story. It is also a post-hoc rationalization constructed after seeing six correct predictions. It should be treated as a candidate mechanism, not as an explanation.

**What distinguishes a mechanism from a post-hoc rationalization:**

A mechanism generates predictions that differ from the predictions generated by the regularity alone. The regularity predicts: High maturity → APG, Low maturity → Watch B. A mechanism would predict: *because* causal-role uncertainty decreases as identification capacity increases, there should be a continuous relationship — domains at the edge of the Low/Intermediate boundary should show Watch B weakening before APG becomes dominant; domains with high NEA but low DAM should show different object patterns than domains with high DAM but low NEA.

None of these granular predictions have been tested. Testing them is what distinguishes mechanism-discovery from continued pattern observation.

## 4. Fork A vs. Fork B — Cycle 7 Decision

### Fork A — Continued Replication

**What it does:** Run additional domains under the operationalization regime. Accumulate more data points for the gradient pattern.

**What it answers:** Whether the gradient generalizes to additional domain types not yet tested. Tests whether the current six-domain sample is representative or domain-specific.

**Candidate domains:** RC-041 could use any scored domain. Microfinance (Candidate C, Intermediate) would add a second Intermediate replication. A new Low-maturity domain not in the original candidate set (e.g., political science, literary criticism, historical causation) would stress-test the Low end.

**Marginal value:** Diminishing. The seventh confirmation adds less than the third. The gradient is confirmed at all three levels under two measurement regimes. Additional confirmations do not close the mechanism gap — they widen the unexplained regularity.

**Risk:** Drift. If RC-041 through RC-045 are all confirmations, the gradient becomes a well-documented pattern with no explanatory progress. The repository accumulates evidence about a thing it cannot explain.

### Fork B — Mechanism Discovery

**What it does:** Ask what process connects literature maturity to bottleneck dominance. Attempt to derive predictions that the mechanism makes but the regularity alone does not.

**What it would require:** Identifying a process-level account (causal-identification capacity as the linking variable); deriving predictions from it that differ from the regularity's predictions (granular criterion interactions; transition-zone behavior); designing audits that test those differential predictions.

**Candidate first step:** Pre-register a granular prediction before the next audit — not just "Intermediate → APG" but "domains with High NEA but Low DAM should show Watch B more weakly than domains with Low NEA and Low DAM." This tests whether the criteria function as independent variables or only as a composite score.

**Risk:** Overfitting. The candidate mechanism (causal-identification capacity) was constructed after seeing the gradient. It fits because it was designed to fit. Mechanism proposals generated post-hoc from confirmed regularities are hard to distinguish from rationalizations until they produce predictions that could have been wrong.

**Safeguard:** Treat mechanism proposals as candidates, not promoted objects. Pre-register granular predictions before testing them. Same discipline as RSQ-001 and OQ-Maturity.

## 5. What Cycle 7 Should Decide

Not which fork is correct — the repository does not know yet. But which fork to pursue, and why.

The repository has accumulated enough of a pattern that continued replication will not tell it anything new about whether the pattern exists. It will only tell it whether the pattern generalizes further. That is a lower-information activity than asking what produces the pattern.

The mechanism question is now tractable in a way it was not after RC-035. After RC-035, only one observation existed at each maturity level; a mechanism would have been pure speculation. After RC-040, six observations across two eras with independent predictor measurement give a mechanism proposal something to be measured against.

**Recommendation for Cycle 7:** Fork B — mechanism-discovery orientation, with Fork A as the vehicle. Design audits that test granular mechanism predictions rather than replicating the pattern at the level already confirmed. Continue running audits (Fork A is operationally necessary), but choose domains that stress-test the mechanism's granular predictions rather than simply adding data points to the confirmed pattern.

This is not a plan. It is the decision the repository should make explicitly before Cycle 7 begins, rather than making by default.
