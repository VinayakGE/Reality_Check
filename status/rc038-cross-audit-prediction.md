## Prediction

**Domain:** Macroeconomics / Monetary Policy

**Claim:** "The aggressive monetary tightening by the U.S. Federal Reserve in the early 1980s was the primary cause of the reduction in inflation." — Paul Volcker / Federal Reserve / mainstream macroeconomic consensus

**Predicted Primary Object:** Watch B

**Predicted Secondary Object:** APG — present, but conditional on Watch B's partial resolution; not the primary frontier

**Predicted Non-Appearance:** Counterfactual Unavailability

**Reason:**

Low-maturity classification across all four criteria predicts Watch B dominance. The mechanistic disputes in monetary economics are not primarily about contribution shares — they are about whether monetary tightening was causally necessary for the disinflation at all. The fiscal theory of the price level (Sargent, Leeper, Cochrane) offers a structural alternative: that the disinflation required a credible fiscal adjustment alongside monetary tightening, and that monetary tightening alone in a fiscal-dominant regime produces only recession, not sustained price stability. Supply-side accounts (oil price reversal, global commodity cycle) offer an alternative timing mechanism that questions whether the 1983 recovery was a monetary success or the unwinding of an external shock.

These are Watch B disputes. They are not asking "how much did Volcker's tightening contribute?" — they are asking "did it causally produce the disinflation, rather than the disinflation happening for other structural reasons?"

APG is present as a secondary object: if causal contribution is granted, the shares among monetary tightening, credibility/expectations effects, fiscal adjustment, oil price reversal, and global factors are genuinely disputed. But APG's activation requires partial Watch B resolution, and Watch B is not resolved here.

Counterfactual Unavailability is predicted absent because the Volcker episode is a reasonably sharp policy intervention with identifiable timing, and counterfactual macroeconomic models (VAR, DSGE) are routinely used to simulate counterfactual paths. The counterfactual is contested, not structurally unavailable.

## What Would Constitute a Real Failure

**APG primary, Watch B secondary:** The hypothesis takes a direct hit. Low-maturity classification predicts Watch B dominance; if the live frontier is contribution shares rather than causal presence, either (a) the maturity criteria failed to detect mature causal identification in this domain, or (b) the hypothesis is wrong about the Low → Watch B mapping.

**Watch B entirely absent:** Implausible given the fiscal theory literature, but would mean monetary policy's causal role is so settled that even Low-maturity criteria missed it. Would require re-examining DAM and SRC scores.

**New object appears:** Possible; macroeconomics has methodological disputes not present in epidemiology or climate science (identification via VAR exclusion restrictions, DSGE model dependence).
