# RC-036: Pacific Northwest Heat Dome Attribution

**Claim:** "Anthropogenic climate change significantly increased the probability and severity of the 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome."

**Sources:** World Weather Attribution (WWA) Collaborative — van Oldenborgh et al. (2021), "Attribution of the 2021 western North America heat wave"; Supplementary findings from National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and multiple independent attribution groups.

**Key formulations:**
- WWA (2021): "The occurrence of a heatwave with maximum temperatures as observed in the Pacific Northwest in 2021 has been made at least 150 times more likely due to human-induced climate change."
- WWA (2021): "The event was virtually impossible without climate change."
- Additional finding: The observed temperatures were roughly 2°C hotter than what pre-industrial climate conditions would have produced.

**Status:** Unresolved
**Date:** 2026-06-24

---

## Why This Claim Matters

The 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome was the most extreme heat event in recorded history for the region. Temperatures in Lytton, British Columbia reached 49.6°C (121.3°F) — nearly 5°C above previous records. Approximately 600–800 excess deaths occurred in the Pacific Northwest over the three-day event. The question of whether and how much anthropogenic climate change contributed is consequential for climate liability, adaptation policy, and public understanding of climate risk.

This claim also represents the first Reality Check audit in climate science, chosen specifically to test whether the repository's ontology — developed primarily through development economics, global health, and technology transfer cases — transfers to a domain with fundamentally different methodological apparatus.

## Alternative Explanations

**H1 — Attribution Science Resolution (APG framing)**

Anthropogenic climate change is an established causal contributor to the 2021 heat dome. The WWA attribution study and subsequent analyses used counterfactual climate modeling to establish causal presence. The remaining scientific questions concern magnitude: how much of the specific temperature anomaly (+4.9°C above average maximum at the regional level) is attributable to long-term anthropogenic forcing versus natural variability, internal atmospheric dynamics (the omega block pattern), and land surface feedbacks? This is a contribution-share dispute, not a causal-presence dispute.

*Key prediction:* The live scientific debate is structured around decomposing the contributions of (a) long-term warming trend, (b) the specific omega block configuration, (c) antecedent soil moisture deficit and heat island effects — not around whether anthropogenic forcing contributed at all.

**H2 — Causal Role Uncertainty (Watch B framing)**

"Climate change made this event more probable" is not equivalent to "climate change caused this event." The 2021 heat dome required a specific atmospheric configuration (stationary high-pressure ridge, blocking omega pattern) that could have occurred in a pre-industrial climate, though with lower probability. Critics argue that the attribution methodology conflates probability increase with causation: a 150x probability increase still means the event was possible without climate change. Extreme tail events require multiple concurrent factors; attributing them to any single factor — even one that shifted probabilities substantially — may exceed what the evidence supports.

This is a Watch B-type dispute: not about contribution share, but about whether "cause" is the appropriate description of the relationship between anthropogenic forcing and a specific event that required other conditions to materialize.

**H3 — Natural Variability Primacy**

Some climatologists and atmospheric scientists argue the 2021 heat dome was primarily a result of unusual atmospheric dynamics (the strength and persistence of the omega blocking pattern) that operate at timescales largely independent of long-term warming trends. Climate change may have "loaded the dice" toward more extreme heat events, but the specific magnitude of this event was dominated by internal variability that would have produced a record heat wave even under lower background temperatures. Attribution science studies that find 150x probability increase are modeling the tail of a distribution; the extreme tail behavior may be more sensitive to internal variability than long-term forced warming.

*This explanation is not equivalent to denying climate change's contribution.* It argues that the specific event magnitude was dominated by internal dynamics, not that anthropogenic warming was absent or irrelevant.

## Evidence Supporting the Claim

**WWA attribution study (van Oldenborgh et al. 2021):** The World Weather Attribution Collaborative analyzed the event using multiple climate models and observational records. Their central finding: a heat wave of this magnitude was "virtually impossible" without human-induced climate change, and climate change made it at least 150 times more likely. The analysis used both observational trend analysis and climate model counterfactual runs (comparing present-day climate simulations to pre-industrial control runs).

**Temperature anomaly magnitude:** The recorded temperatures in Lytton were not merely record-breaking by small margins — they exceeded previous records by approximately 4.6°C. For context, past Pacific Northwest heat events had never exceeded about 45°C; 49.6°C represents a discontinuity that attribution scientists argue requires additional forcing beyond internal variability.

**CMIP6 model agreement:** Multiple climate models (from the CMIP6 ensemble) agree that the probability of events like the 2021 heat dome has increased substantially under observed anthropogenic forcing. The agreement across independent models using different physical parameterizations strengthens the attribution claim.

**Long-term trend corroboration:** Pacific Northwest summer temperatures have shown statistically significant warming trends over the instrumental record, consistent with global patterns of anthropogenic forcing. The attribution is not based solely on the single event but on patterns consistent with the broader forced warming signal.

**Dead reckoning comparison:** Several attribution groups noted that the observed temperatures exceeded what existing climate model distributions would have predicted even in present-day conditions — possibly suggesting models were underestimating warming effects in this region. This cuts toward the claim being conservative rather than overstated.

## Evidence Against / Creating Doubt

**The omega blocking event:** The immediate meteorological cause of the heat dome was an unusually persistent and intense omega blocking pattern (stationary high-pressure ridge) in the upper atmosphere. These patterns are not directly forced by anthropogenic warming and may be influenced by natural modes of variability (Pacific Decadal Oscillation, ENSO). Attribution studies must grapple with the fact that the specific synoptic configuration was a necessary co-cause — and whether anthropogenic warming is the explanation for the configuration's intensity or merely amplified temperatures within a pattern driven by other dynamics.

*This is a critical APG dimension:* Multiple concurrent causes (anthropogenic forcing + specific atmospheric dynamics + antecedent land conditions) all contributed. Their relative shares are not cleanly decomposable.

**Model underperformance at the tail:** The WWA study itself noted that the observed temperatures exceeded what current climate model ensembles would have predicted even with present-day forcing. This creates a methodological complication: if models underestimate the probability of such events, the attribution framing ("at least 150x more likely") may be based on an imprecise baseline. The probability ratio calculation depends on model accuracy in the extreme tail, which is precisely where model uncertainty is highest.

**"Virtually impossible" framing complexity:** The claim that the event was "virtually impossible" without climate change is based on the probability ratio exceeding 150x — meaning less than a 0.67% probability per year without climate change. This framing invites Watch B-type reinterpretation: a 0.67% annual probability is not zero. If the event could have occurred (at very low probability) in pre-industrial conditions, is "caused by climate change" the right description, or is "dramatically more probable due to climate change" the more accurate framing? This is the probabilistic causation threshold question — distinct from both Watch B (cause disputed) and APG (shares disputed).

**Attribution science model dependence:** Attribution results depend on the choice of climate models, the baseline period, the specific event definition (how the "heat wave" is defined operationally), and the statistical methodology. Different methodological choices produce different probability ratios. Some analyses find lower ratios. The uncertainty range matters for how strong the attribution claim can be.

**The compound event problem:** The heat dome severity was amplified by concurrent factors: (a) severe antecedent drought reducing evaporative cooling capacity, (b) urban heat island effects in affected cities, (c) the specific topographic configuration of the Pacific Northwest amplifying temperatures in inland valleys. Decomposing climate change's contribution from these concurrent amplifying factors is precisely the APG problem: each factor is accepted as contributing, but contribution shares are not cleanly separable.

## Missing Evidence

**High-resolution attribution for mortality:** The ~600–800 excess deaths are the human cost metric, but the attribution of mortality to temperature versus other factors (lack of air conditioning, age/vulnerability distribution) involves a separate causal chain from the temperature attribution. The repository's existing APG framework (contribution shares among concurrent factors) applies to this second-order attribution problem as well.

**Systematic multi-model uncertainty quantification:** The probability ratio range (at least 150x, possibly much higher) reflects model uncertainty. Better quantification of the upper tail of the probability ratio distribution would help determine whether "at least 150x" is a tight lower bound or a much looser one.

**Attribution decomposition by contributing factor:** No published analysis cleanly separates the contributions of (1) long-term anthropogenic warming trend, (2) land surface moisture deficit, (3) urban heat island amplification, and (4) the specific blocking event configuration to the final temperature anomaly. This decomposition would speak directly to the APG bottleneck.

## Current Status and Confidence

**Status: Unresolved**

The attribution science literature has substantially resolved the Watch B question: anthropogenic climate change is an accepted causal contributor to the probability and severity of the 2021 heat dome. The primary remaining bottleneck is APG: decomposing the contributions of anthropogenic forcing, atmospheric dynamics, and concurrent land/urban amplifying factors.

However, a third bottleneck appeared that was flagged as a candidate in the pre-registration: the **probabilistic causation threshold**. The debate about whether "150x more probable" constitutes "caused" for policy and legal purposes is neither Watch B (causal role disputed in scientific terms) nor APG (contribution shares disputed). It is a dispute about what probability ratio constitutes adequate attribution for non-scientific purposes — a threshold question that exists at the boundary between scientific findings and their policy interpretation.

**Confidence:** The Watch B question is close to resolved in scientific literature. The APG question is active and unlikely to resolve without substantially better high-resolution attribution studies. The probabilistic causation threshold question may never fully resolve because it is partly normative, not empirical.

## Watch Item Assessment

**APG — Primary:** Confirmed as primary epistemic object in the scientific literature. The core scientific disputes concern contribution magnitude (how much of the severity is attributable to anthropogenic forcing vs. concurrent co-causes), not causal role. This confirms the pre-registration prediction.

**Watch B — Secondary, specific location:** Watch B does not dominate the scientific debate but appears in public and policy discourse, and in a specific scientific sub-question: whether "virtually impossible without climate change" translates correctly to "caused by climate change" given the probabilistic framing. The scientific literature has largely resolved Watch B for this event; the public/policy discourse has not.

**Sequential structure test:** Where Watch B and APG appear for the same causal question (does climate change explain the heat dome?), the sequential structure holds: Watch B is provisionally resolved by attribution science, enabling APG to become the operative frontier. The audit applied the sequential/parallel distinction naturally without needing to re-derive it.

**Counterfactual Unavailability — Absent:** As predicted. Attribution science explicitly constructs the counterfactual world through climate modeling. The counterfactual is modeled, not unavailable.

**Probabilistic Causation Threshold — New candidate object:**

The audit surfaced a dispute structure not cleanly covered by existing repository objects:

*What it is:* Agreement that cause A substantially increased the probability of outcome B (accepted causal presence, accepted contribution), combined with dispute about whether a specific probability ratio constitutes sufficient grounds to describe A as having "caused" B — particularly for policy and legal purposes.

*Why it differs from Watch B:* Watch B disputes whether cause A had any causal role. The probabilistic causation threshold dispute accepts that A increased probability substantially and disputes whether that probability increase constitutes causation.

*Why it differs from APG:* APG disputes the contribution share of accepted causes. The probabilistic causation threshold accepts the probability ratio but disputes whether probability ratios are the right frame for causal attribution at all.

*One instance.* Cannot be promoted. Recorded as candidate for tracking.

**Cross-audit prediction result:** APG confirmed as primary. Watch B confirmed as secondary in limited scope. Counterfactual Unavailability absent as predicted. Probabilistic Causation Threshold appeared as flagged candidate. Pre-registration held across all four specified dimensions.
