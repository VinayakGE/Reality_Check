# Reality Check #007

## Claim

> "Bitcoin will hit $100,000 by the end of 2024. The spot ETF approval and the April halving are the two catalysts that will take it there."

## Source

- Speaker: Geoff Kendrick (Head of FX and Digital Assets Research)
- Organization: Standard Chartered Bank
- Date: January 2024
- Link: https://www.standardchartered.com/en/stories/all/2024/sc-q4-2024-btc-price-target.html

---

## Why This Matters

This is the second already-resolved audit. The first (RC-006) produced Not Supported and revealed that the template lacked a status category. RC-007 was chosen to test whether the template behaves the same way when the outcome is positive. Bitcoin reached approximately $103,000 in December 2024. The claim resolved in favor of the prediction. The question is not whether the claim was right — it was — but whether being right for the stated reasons and being right for different reasons are meaningfully different outcomes, and whether the template can express that difference.

---

## Alternative Explanations

### H1 — The claim is correct and the mechanism is correct
The spot Bitcoin ETF approvals in January 2024 (BlackRock, Fidelity, and others) brought institutional capital that had previously been blocked. The April halving reduced new supply entering the market. The combination of increased demand and reduced supply produced the predicted price level. The two catalysts Kendrick named were the two catalysts that drove the outcome. The claim was right for the stated reasons.

### H2 — The claim is correct but the mechanism is wrong
Bitcoin reached $100K primarily because of post-US election macro dynamics in November–December 2024: a pro-crypto incoming administration, expectations of lighter regulatory treatment, and a risk-on environment following the election outcome. The ETF and halving effects were priced in earlier in 2024 (Bitcoin reached ~$73K in March 2024, near the halving). The final move to $100K was driven by political factors that were not part of Kendrick's stated thesis. The prediction was right; the explanation was wrong.

### H3 — The claim is correct but coincidental
Bitcoin's price is sufficiently volatile and sentiment-driven that a $100K target set with a 12-month horizon in January 2024 was more likely to be met than not, given that Bitcoin was already at ~$42K and had a history of 2–3x moves in bull years. The catalysts Kendrick named are reasonable post-hoc explanations, but they are selected from a set of many possible narratives. A prediction of $100K in a known Bitcoin bull year is a low-precision claim that was likely to resolve positively regardless of the specific mechanism.

---

## Evidence Supporting the Claim

- Bitcoin reached approximately $103,400 on December 5, 2024, confirming the price target within the stated timeframe
- US spot Bitcoin ETFs launched January 11, 2024, and accumulated over $50 billion in net assets within their first year — a scale of institutional demand that had no prior precedent in Bitcoin's history
- The Bitcoin halving occurred April 20, 2024, reducing daily new supply from approximately 900 BTC/day to 450 BTC/day, consistent with the supply-side mechanism Kendrick cited
- BlackRock's IBIT ETF became one of the fastest ETFs in history to reach $10 billion in assets, confirming that institutional demand was real, not anticipated and already priced

## Evidence Against the Claim (as a precise causal claim)

- Bitcoin reached a local high of approximately $73,000 in March 2024 — before the halving, and immediately following ETF approval — then declined to approximately $54,000 by August 2024, suggesting the ETF and halving catalysts had exhausted their immediate price effect
- The move from ~$63,000 to $103,000 occurred primarily in November–December 2024, following the US presidential election, which is not among the two catalysts Kendrick named
- Multiple other analysts also predicted $100K in 2024 using different mechanisms (macro liquidity cycle, global M2 growth, retail FOMO cycle) — the correct outcome does not distinguish which causal explanation is right
- Standard Chartered raised their Bitcoin target further to $200K during 2024, suggesting their internal model was updating on factors beyond the original two catalysts

---

## Missing Evidence

Less than RC-006, but not empty.

- Attribution data on which investor flows drove the November–December 2024 move: if post-election flows were primarily ETF-driven, H1 gains support; if they were OTC and futures-driven (typically institutional directional bets), H2 gains support
- Whether the halving had a measurable supply-side price effect distinguishable from concurrent demand increases — the two mechanisms are difficult to isolate in price data

The Missing Evidence section is not empty this time. The outcome is known; the mechanism is partially unknown. This is different from RC-006, where outcome and mechanism were both resolved.

**First Supported audit.**

The claim stated a price target ($100K) by a date (end of 2024). Bitcoin reached that price within that timeframe. The claim is Supported.

The more precise question — whether it was Supported for the stated reasons (ETF + halving) or for different reasons (post-election macro) — cannot be fully resolved from available data. ## Confidence

**High (on the price claim) / Low (on the mechanism claim)**

Reason: This audit exposes a distinction the template does not currently capture. A claim can have two components — a prediction (Bitcoin hits $100K) and an explanation (because of ETF + halving) — that resolve differently. The prediction is Supported at High confidence. The explanation is Unresolved at Low confidence. The current Status field cannot express both simultaneously.

RC-006 found that the template lacked a "Not Supported" status. RC-007 finds something narrower: the template treats a claim as a single unit, but some claims contain a prediction and a causal explanation, which may resolve independently.

## Review Trigger

None for the price prediction — it resolved. The mechanism question (H1 vs. H2) would be clarified by academic or institutional post-mortems on 2024 Bitcoin price drivers, which typically appear 12–18 months after the fact in financial research.

## Open Questions

1. RC-006 surfaced a missing status (Not Supported). RC-007 surfaces a different gap: when a claim contains both a prediction and an explanation, and the prediction resolves but the explanation does not, what is the Status? Supported? Partially Supported? The current three-option system (plus Not Supported) cannot express this.
2. Does it matter whether the claim was right for the right reasons? If an analyst's price target is met but via a different mechanism than stated, did the analysis succeed? Reality Check currently has no way to distinguish "correct prediction" from "correct prediction + correct mechanism."
3. H3 (the claim was likely to resolve positively given Bitcoin's historical volatility) is the most uncomfortable explanation. It implies that a confident causal prediction was indistinguishable from a lucky range estimate. How should Reality Check treat high-volatility assets where specific predictions frequently resolve positively by chance?
